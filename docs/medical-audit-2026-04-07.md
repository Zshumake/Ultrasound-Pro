## P1-01 · `wrist-dequervain` · Wrong needle corridor direction

**File:** `assets/data/hand.json` (1st dorsal compartment, De Quervain's)
**Section:** `corridor` → line ~134

**Current wording:**
> "Often volar → dorsal path into sheath: skin/fat (above), sheath (below)"

**Concern:** A volar-to-dorsal trajectory at the radial styloid passes directly through (or immediately adjacent to) the **radial artery** and **superficial branch of the radial nerve**. The first dorsal compartment is on the **dorsoradial** aspect of the wrist; standard published technique enters from the **dorsal or dorsoradial** side, NOT volar. Most atlases describe a distal-to-proximal or volar-to-dorsal-AT-AN-ANGLE-FROM-THE-DORSAL-SIDE orientation. The plain-English reading of "volar → dorsal" implies entering from the palm side, which is unsafe. [CITATION: ____]

**Proposed wording:**
> "Dorsal in-plane approach: enter from the dorsal/dorsoradial aspect distal-to-proximal, advancing into the sheath superficial to the APL/EPB tendons. Avoid any volar trajectory — the radial artery lies on the volar side of the radial styloid."

## P1-02 · `hip-piriformis` · Contradictory direction wording

**File:** `assets/data/hip.json` (Piriformis)
**Section:** `pearls` → line ~256

**Current wording:**
> "Approach lateral-to-medial to keep the needle away from the sciatic notch."

**Concern:** The greater sciatic notch is the **medial** boundary where the sciatic nerve exits the pelvis. A lateral-to-medial trajectory drives the needle **toward** the notch, not away. The piriformis runs sacrum (medial) → greater trochanter (lateral). Most published techniques describe **medial-to-lateral** in-plane approach so the needle terminates lateral to the sciatic and lateral to the inferior gluteal vessels. The current wording is internally contradictory.

Note: there is a competing school that prefers a lateral-to-medial belly entry to engage the muscle perpendicular to its fibers. Either is defensible IF the rationale is correct — but the *stated reason* ("away from the sciatic notch") is anatomically wrong. [CITATION: ____]

**Proposed wording (option A — change direction):**
> "Approach medial-to-lateral so the needle terminates lateral to the sciatic nerve and the inferior gluteal vessels."

**Proposed wording (option B — keep direction, fix rationale):**
> "Approach lateral-to-medial through the gluteus maximus into the piriformis belly. Always keep the sciatic nerve in view at the deep/inferior border and stop the needle within the muscle belly — do not advance toward the medial sciatic notch."

## P1-04 · `ankle-ttj` · Approach direction may walk into NV bundle

**File:** `assets/data/foot.json` (Tibiotalar joint)
**Section:** `corridor` and `pearls`

**Current wording:**
> Corridor: "Medial → lateral path deep to anterior tendons (above) and superficial to capsule/cartilage (below)"
> Pearls: "Advance medial-to-lateral deep to the anterior tendons."
> Avoid: "Deep peroneal nerve and dorsalis pedis artery"

**Concern:** The dorsalis pedis artery and deep peroneal nerve run between the **EHL and EDL** tendons — i.e., on the **lateral** side of the anterior ankle compartment. A medial-to-lateral trajectory advances the needle tip *toward* the NV bundle. > Corridor: "Anteromedial entry, **medial to the tibialis anterior tendon**, in-plane along the medial gutter. Keep the deep peroneal nerve and dorsalis pedis artery (lying between EHL and EDL on the lateral side) **out of the needle path** — do not advance toward the lateral compartment."
> Pearls: "Enter medial to tibialis anterior. The DPA/DPN bundle lies *lateral* to EHL — stay medial of it throughout."

## P1-05 · `foot-mortons-neuroma` · Mulder's maneuver described incorrectly

**File:** `assets/data/foot.json` (Morton's neuroma)
**Section:** `landmarking` and `pearls`

**Current wording:**
> Landmarking: "Visual Cue: The 'Mulder's Click' — apply **plantar-to-dorsal pressure** to displace the neuroma between metatarsal heads."
> Pearls: "Use the plantar 'squeeze test' (Mulder's maneuver)…"

**Concern:** Mulder's maneuver/sign is **medial-lateral compression** of the forefoot (squeezing the metatarsal heads together from the sides) while applying simultaneous plantar pressure on the interspace — eliciting a palpable/audible click as the neuroma displaces dorsally. It is NOT plantar-to-dorsal pressure alone. A resident reading the current wording would not know to perform the technique correctly, and may fail to elicit the dynamic finding that confirms target identification. [CITATION: Mulder JD. *J Bone Joint Surg Br* 1951;33-B:94-95]

**Proposed wording:**
> Landmarking: "**Mulder's Maneuver:** Squeeze the forefoot transversely (medial-lateral compression of the metatarsal heads) while simultaneously applying plantar pressure on the suspect interspace. Watch in real-time for the hypoechoic neuroma to 'pop' dorsally between the metatarsal heads — this is the sonographic Mulder's click and confirms target identification."
> Pearls: "Mulder's = medial-lateral squeeze + plantar push. Don't rely on plantar-only pressure."

## P1-06 · `wrist-carpal` · Particulate steroid + high lidocaine volume near median nerve

**File:** `assets/data/hand.json` (Carpal tunnel)
**Section:** `supplies`

**Current wording:**
> "1% Lidocaine (2-5 mL)"
> "1cc Kenalog or Dexamethasone"

**Concern (two issues):**
1. **Steroid choice:** Most modern guidance for perineural injections **strongly prefers non-particulate steroid** (dexamethasone) when injecting near a nerve, due to risk of intraneural particulate embolization causing infarction. Listing Kenalog (triamcinolone, particulate) as the first option is contrary to current recommendations from AIUM, AMSSM, and the AAPMR. Most carpal tunnel protocols default to **dexamethasone** or **methylprednisolone** as first choice. [CITATION: ____]
2. **Volume:** 2–5 mL of lidocaine in the carpal tunnel is at the upper limit. Standard total injectate volume (anesthetic + steroid) for the carpal tunnel is typically **1–3 mL total**. Excess volume increases pressure within an already-tight compartment and may transiently worsen median nerve compression. [CITATION: ____]

**Proposed wording:**
> Supplies:
> - "1% Lidocaine (1-2 mL)"
> - "Dexamethasone 4-10 mg (preferred — non-particulate, safer near nerve) OR methylprednisolone 40 mg"
> Pearls (add): "Total injectate volume in the carpal tunnel should be ≤ 3 mL to avoid further nerve compression."

## P1-07 · `shoulder-biceps` · Particulate steroid in tendon sheath risk

**File:** `assets/data/shoulder.json` (Long head biceps)
**Section:** `supplies` + `avoid`

**Current wording:**

> Landmarking: "Long-axis sagittal probe over the suprapatellar recess."
> Corridor: "In-plane **superior-to-inferior** (cranial entry along the probe long-axis) OR out-of-plane lateral walk-down."
